About Varki

A Beloved South Indian Tradition

Varki is a cherished South Indian tea shop snack that has been delighting taste buds for generations. This crispy, golden delicacy is crafted with care using traditional methods passed down through families.

In the bustling tea shops of South India, Varki is served alongside steaming cups of chai, creating a perfect harmony of flavors. The satisfying crunch and subtle spice notes make it an irresistible companion to the rich, milky tea.

At Varnis Varki, we preserve this cultural heritage by creating authentic Varki using time-honored techniques and the finest ingredients. Each piece is a celebration of South Indian culinary tradition.

The Art of Making Varki

1

Preparing the Dough

Rice flour is mixed with carefully measured spices, salt, and water to create a smooth, consistent dough that will yield the perfect texture.

2

Rolling & Shaping

The dough is rolled into thin sheets and cut into the distinctive Varki shape, a process requiring skilled hands and years of experience.

3

Traditional Frying

Each piece is fried in clarified butter at the perfect temperature until it achieves the signature golden color and crispy texture.

4

Cooling & Packaging

The freshly fried Varki is allowed to cool before being carefully packaged to preserve its crispness and flavor for your enjoyment.
